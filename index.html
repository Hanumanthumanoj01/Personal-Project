Chapter Two

When You Left, You Took a Piece of This House With You

July came, and with it — suitcases. Two of them. Quietly standing by the door like they knew they were carrying more than clothes.

Canada. A country I've only seen in photographs. Cold winters. Maple leaves. Streets that don't know your name yet. And you two — landing there with nothing but each other and everything you needed.

Since then, I haven't seen your faces except on a screen. I haven't heard Bawa's laugh bounce off the walls of this house. I haven't fought with you over the remote, Akka. And honestly — the house has been a little quieter. A little less alive.

I miss you both. More than I will ever say out loud. But I've never been more proud of you — building something beautiful, far from home, with only love to keep you warm on the cold days.
